Reflection 1: First Week of USERDES Class

In our user design class, it has been mostly light-weighted tasks. The Trello board was really funny most of the time because of my classmates, trolling all over the board. The class is very simple, where you can express your ideas freely with only a few restraints. I can say that this is a great as our first subject of the morning, and for the whole week. Our outputs can differ from each other and can vary depending on our own perspectives. I can also learn the different possible answers on our other activities through our class sharing our ideas at the front which lets me see that there are actually other ways which I have not to think of even once when I answered the activities.

Most of what I learned is how to appreciate the importance of design. Especially in my field, I need to learn these for handling and creating software. For so long I have neglected the whole design of the user interface I use on my phone, laptop, and computers that I use almost daily. Design is not only for aesthetics, but it can be applied in many aspects. You can use design to enable for more productive way, or where it is more comfortable for the user. You can also use design to manipulate the users. Changing the color of a simple button could massively affect how many times the user would press the said button. In conclusion, user design changed my over all perspective not only on computer user interfaces, but also all the things we use and see every day, from the clothes I wear and all the buildings I see in the city.
